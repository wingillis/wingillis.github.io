Background

Back in 2012, I followed the newly constructed and free Codecademy course that taught the fundamentals of programming through javascript. The lessons were sparse and short, but enough to fuel my excitement to explore the new world of possibilities software development provided.

Over the years, I’ve developed experience programming with the following languages, listed in order of comfort level: 1) python, 2) javascript, 3) julia, 4) c, 5) matlab, and have dabbled in other languages such as lua, clojure, groovy, c# and go. There are similarities many languages share making it easier to take up a new language, but also differences that make some languages a better choice to solve certain problems. I’ve had fun thinking about how languages and their accompanying frameworks are best suited to solve different problems.

I’ve continued to practice software engineering and other programming-adjacent skills in college and graduate school.

Hobby projects
